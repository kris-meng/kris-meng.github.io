Who I am

I'm an ML researcher and engineer with experience across neuroscience, healthcare, and applied AI. My work includes self-supervised models for clinical biosignals, time-series forecasting, computer vision (ViTs), and LLM-based projects. My master's thesis focused on improving sleep stage classification for Parkinson's patients, and I've also contributed to mental health chatbots and replicated ML models from papers end-to-end.

I thrive in interdisciplinary teams and enjoy translating research into practical, real-world solutions — whether in biomedical AI, computational biology, or broader ML applications like forecasting, vision, or natural language.
