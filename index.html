// 01 — about

Who I am

I'm a machine learning researcher and engineer with a background spanning neuroscience, cognitive science, and computer science. My work focuses on applying deep learning to biosignal data — particularly EEG and DBS recordings — to advance clinical understanding of the brain.

I recently completed a thesis-based Master's in Computer Science at York University, where I developed self-supervised foundation models for sleep-stage classification in Parkinson's disease patients.

I'm based in Toronto and open to new opportunities in ML engineering and research.
